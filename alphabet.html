The Vowels

Vowels in Farsi - ah, uh, e, i, o, ooh, ey and ow - are formed by placing markers on letters. This includes three short vowels, three long vowels and two other vowels. Click to listen below:

ـُ
pronounced as /o/
ـِ
pronounced as /e/
ـَ
pronounced as /ah/
او
pronounced as /ooh/
ای
pronounced as /i/
آ
pronounced as /uh/
ـُوْ
pronounced as /ow/
ـِیْ
pronounced as /ey/

When reading Farsi, several vowels may be unwritten in each word. For example, گربه (cat /gorbeh/) is made up of guf, re, be and he but contains no vowel markers. However, it has implied vowels /o/ and /e/. This gives this word the pronunciation /gorbeh/.

This website writes all its Farsi words with vowel markers. However, it's important to remember that in general use they are not used, and fluent readers are expected to learn vowel sounds for every word.

Another marker used is tashdid (ـّ), which adds an extra consonant to the letter, making it sound twice as long.
